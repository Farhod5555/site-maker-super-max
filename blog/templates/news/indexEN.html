WE ARE THE FEDERATION OF CONSUMER RIGHTS PROTECTION SOCIETIES

  • OUR ACTIVITY - Since 1999

The consumer rights protection movement in the Republic of Uzbekistan In accordance with Article 30 of the Law “On Consumer Rights Protection” adopted by the Oliy Majlis of the Republic of Uzbekistan on April 26, 1996, in 1997, at the initiative of a number of like-minded people who decided to contribute to the protection of the population of Uzbekistan from low-quality products and services and combine their capabilities, the consumer rights protection social movement was established in the Shaykhantohur district of our capital in the form of the Consumer Rights Protection Society of Uzbekistan and was registered as the Republican Consumer Rights Protection Society in 1999. At that time, territorial societies were established in 7 regions of our republic. In 2000, at the Extraordinary II Congress of the Consumer Rights Protection Society of Uzbekistan, the society was renamed the Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies of Uzbekistan. At that time, societies were established in 9 regions and the city of Tashkent. Thanks to the efforts of active consumers, this public organization received special attention and support from the state. The adoption of Resolution No. 414 of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan "On Measures to Expand Public Participation in the Protection of Consumer Rights" of November 28, 2002 marked a new stage in the development of this social movement. At the same time, this resolution approved the organizational structure of consumer rights protection societies and the structure of the executive apparatus of the Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies. The III Constituent Conference of the Federation on January 17, 2003 united territorial associations in the Federation of the Republic of Karakalpakstan, the city of Tashkent, and 12 regions of the republic.

RULE:

1. General conditions

1

The Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies of Uzbekistan (hereinafter referred to as the “Federation”) is a self-governing non-governmental non-profit organization voluntarily established by individuals with the aim of introducing effective mechanisms for increasing the level of legal protection of consumers through the comprehensive development of the social movement for the protection of consumer rights in Uzbekistan. Its organizational and legal form is a public association.

2

The Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies of Uzbekistan was established on the basis of the Constitution of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the laws "On Non-Governmental Non-Profit Organizations", "On Public Associations in the Republic of Uzbekistan" and "On Consumer Rights Protection", as well as the Resolution of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. 414 dated November 28, 2002 "On Measures to Expand Public Participation in the Protection of Consumer Rights", as well as other legislative acts regulating the activities of non-governmental non-profit organizations.

3

The Federation and its organizational territorial structures carry out their activities on the basis of the current legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan and this Charter. This Charter is the only constituent document for the Federation and its organizational territorial structures, consumer rights protection societies and their territorial associations.

4

The Federation and its organizational and territorial structures shall acquire the status of a legal entity from the moment of registration with the relevant justice bodies. The Federation and its organizational and territorial structures shall own, use and manage their own property, have the right to conclude contracts on their own behalf, have property and non-property rights, fulfill obligations, participate as a plaintiff and defendant in economic, civil and other courts, have their own accounting balance sheet, round seal and square stamp, and operate by opening soum and currency accounts in banks of the Republic of Uzbekistan. The Federation and its organizational and territorial structures shall be liable for their obligations within the limits of the property and assets belonging to them, and these properties and assets may be managed in the form of payment and collection in accordance with the current legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan. The Federation and its organizational territorial units are not liable for the obligations of the state and (or) its bodies, and the state, in turn, is not liable for the obligations of the Federation and its organizational territorial units.

5

The Federation carries out its activities throughout the territory of the Republic of Uzbekistan.

6

The Federation has the right to open its representative offices and branches, as well as business structures in accordance with the procedure established by current legislation and specified in this Charter, in order to implement the objectives of the Charter. Representative offices and branches, business structures operate in accordance with this Charter, the Regulations on representative offices and branches, business structures.

7

Name of the Federation: In Uzbek: “Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies of Uzbekistan”, abbreviated as “O’IHHQJF”; In Russian: “Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies of Uzbekistan”, abbreviated as “FOZPPU”; In English: “Federation of Consumer Rights Protection Societies of Uzbekistan”, abbreviated as “FSCRPU”.

8

Address of the Federation: Tashkent city – 100011, Navoi street, house 18 A.

9

Everyone can familiarize themselves with the Charter of the Federation.

RULE:

2. Purpose and objectives of the Federation

1

The main goal of the Federation and its organizational territorial structures is to introduce effective mechanisms to increase the level of legal protection of consumers through the comprehensive development of the social movement for the protection of consumer rights in Uzbekistan.

2

The main areas of activity of the Federation and its organizational territorial structures are aimed at protecting the legitimate rights and interests of consumers and include: widespread dissemination of current legislation on the protection of consumer rights among the population; implementation of measures to protect consumer rights and interests; monitoring the necessary quality and safety of consumer goods sold on the domestic market, as well as other requirements of the legislation on the protection of consumer rights, in accordance with the procedure established by law; conducting educational work to improve the consumer culture of the population; working together and interacting with state administration, citizens' self-government bodies, regulatory bodies, and the media in creating effective mechanisms for the protection of consumer rights.

3

The Federation and its organizational territorial structures carry out the following types of activities in order to implement the established areas of their activities: to represent and protect the interests of consumers who are members of the Federation in state, administrative, supervisory and judicial bodies by participating in the implementation of the state policy on the protection of consumer rights and the implementation of the state policy on the regulation of the consumer market; to study and form public opinion on the protection of consumer rights; to increase the level of legal knowledge of consumers by organizing seminars, round tables and meetings, as well as mass events among the population; to provide consumers with truthful information necessary for the correct choice of goods and services; to organize independent expertise on the quality and safety of goods and services; to prepare proposals on programs for regulating prices (tariffs), standards, and quality and safety control systems for goods and services; monitoring the implementation of the requirements of the legislation on the protection of consumer rights and the rules for the provision of trade, household, communal and other services, conducting independent examinations on violations of consumer rights, in accordance with the procedure established by law, and compiling reports on violations of rights; conducting public opinion surveys and social research, surveys to study problems related to the protection of consumer rights with the involvement of relevant specialists; implementing effective mechanisms to ensure the regulation of mutual relations between consumers and manufacturers (seller, service provider) on the principle of warning; warning the population from misleading advertising and protecting them; appealing to enterprises and organizations that have violated the requirements of the legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan on the protection of consumer rights to eliminate violations of legislative requirements; to prepare and submit proposals to authorized state agencies and organizations on improving the quality of goods (works, services) and stopping the production and sale of goods (works, services) that pose a threat to human life, health, property and the environment; to submit materials to the court, prosecutor's office and authorized state agencies on bringing to justice persons guilty of producing and selling products that do not comply with the requirements of consumer protection legislation and established safety requirements; to organize and conduct scientific research and conferences on consumer rights and interests; to organize information provision for its members, exchange of information between them and coordinate their activities; to engage in printing and publishing activities in accordance with the established procedure, as well as to develop and publish periodicals, scientific and popular manuals, posters, booklets, reviews and other publications after obtaining the appropriate permit (license); organize competitions, quizzes, olympiads and other events aimed at identifying and encouraging the most intelligent and talented consumers, the best organizational territorial structure and the best manufacturers of goods (works and services); develop and implement training and retraining programs for employees in the field of consumer rights protection; cooperate with state administration bodies in improving consumer rights protection legislation and ensuring its implementation; develop and implement various projects and programs in cooperation with international public, financial organizations and companies, as well as participate in international consumer organizations; carry out entrepreneurial activities in accordance with the procedure established by law within the framework of the Charter activities aimed at implementing its areas of activity; carry out other types of activities that do not contradict the procedures and rules established by this Charter and legislative acts. Types of activities that require a special permit (license) are carried out after obtaining a special permit (license).

4

The Federation has the following rights: to open its own accounts in banking institutions; to open territorial structures, branches and representative offices, business structures and to be the founder of other legal entities to organize its activities within the framework of this Charter; to conclude transactions and other legal agreements with legal entities and individuals in the territory of the Republic of Uzbekistan and in foreign countries that do not contradict the legislation and this Charter; to participate in courts on its own behalf and on behalf of other persons as a plaintiff and defendant; to construct, purchase, sell, use or lease buildings and structures, equipment, vehicles, inventory, raw materials and other real estate, and to write off its property from the balance sheet; to independently develop and approve its own activity programs and plans; to determine the organizational structure of the Federation, organize the staff of the executive body, determine the amount and form of wages and material incentives for employees; to establish special funds, including at the expense of state financial assistance, to support the social movement for the protection of consumer rights; to involve enterprises, organizations and citizens in their activities on the basis of contracts and other civil law agreements.
